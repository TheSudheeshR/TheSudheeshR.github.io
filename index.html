Research Papers

Analyzing Sentiments Regarding ChatGPT Using Novel BERT: A Machine Learning Approach

Published in MDPI

This study analyzed 21,515 tweets to understand public sentiment towards ChatGPT using the BERT model. The results showed mixed feelings, with many users expressing positive views about its usefulness, while others raised concerns, particularly regarding its suitability for students. Achieving an accuracy of 94.96%, the study provides valuable insights into user perceptions and highlights areas for improvement. Future research will focus on expanding the dataset and exploring ways to ensure responsible AI deployment.

Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers and Deep Learning Model for Analyzing Smartphone-related Tweets

Published in PeerJ

This study presents a BERT-based model for classifying sentiments in smartphone-related tweets, achieving 99% accuracy with preprocessed data. It outperforms several other models, including GRU and LSTM. The research shows that preprocessing improves performance and reduces computation time. The model's insights can help businesses understand public sentiment and improve branding. Future work will explore additional preprocessing techniques to enhance results.
